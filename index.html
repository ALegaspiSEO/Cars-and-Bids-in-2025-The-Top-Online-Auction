What Is Cars & Bids?

Cars & Bids, launched in 2020 by automotive influencer Doug DeMuro, is an online auction platform tailored for modern enthusiast cars from the 1980s to the 2020s. Unlike broader platforms like eBay Motors, it focuses on unique vehicles like Porsches, JDM imports, and quirky models such as the Dodge Dakota Convertible. With over 24,000 auctions completed and a community of 500,000+ registered users, it’s a hotspot for car lovers in 2025. This review explores its features, fees, stylish interface, and whether it’s the best auction site this year.

Key Features of Cars & Bids in 2025

Cars & Bids offers a range of features that make it stand out for enthusiasts:

  • Curated Listings: Focuses on modern classics, ensuring a niche selection of exciting vehicles.
  • Low Fees: Buyers pay a 5% fee (capped at $7,500), and sellers list for free with a $49-$89 fee per auction.
  • Community Engagement: Active comment sections foster trust, with sellers answering buyer questions.
  • SafePay System: Simplifies secure transactions, handling funds and title transfers.
  • Mobile App: A responsive app for iOS and Android enhances bidding and listing on the go.

Doug DeMuro’s YouTube influence (4M+ subscribers) boosts visibility, often featuring listed cars in videos, driving bidding wars.

Pros and Cons of Cars & Bids

How Cars & Bids Compares to Competitors

Let’s compare Cars & Bids to Bring a Trailer, eBay Motors, and Copart:

  • Vs. Bring a Trailer: Cars & Bids focuses on modern cars, has lower fees (5% vs. 5% capped at $5,000), but lacks in-house photography.
  • Vs. eBay Motors: Offers a curated, anti-sniping experience, unlike eBay’s broader, less regulated platform.
  • Vs. Copart: Cars & Bids targets drivable enthusiast cars, while Copart focuses on salvage vehicles, often requiring a dealer’s license.

Cars & Bids excels for modern enthusiast vehicles but may not suit those seeking older classics or project cars.
